Earned Wage Access (EWA) Policy & Employee Acknowledgment

Royal Lee Construction Solutions offers eligible employees access to Earned Wage Access (EWA) as a voluntary financial wellness benefit. This program allows employees to access a portion of wages they have already earned prior to the scheduled payroll date.

1. Program Overview  
Earned Wage Access (EWA) enables employees to request and receive a portion of earned but unpaid wages based on verified hours worked. EWA is not a loan and does not involve interest. It is an advance on earned wages.

2. Eligibility Requirements  
To participate in the EWA program, employees must:
- Be actively employed and in good standing  
- Complete all required onboarding and payroll documentation  
- Have accurately recorded and approved work hours  
- Register and be approved through the company’s designated EWA provider  

3. Access to Funds  
- Employees may request access to earned wages after work hours have been completed and approved by management  
- Access limits may apply based on total hours worked, company policy, and provider guidelines  
- Funds may be delivered via direct deposit, debit card, or provider-supported transfer methods  

4. Fees and Charges  
- Certain transactions, including instant transfers, may include fees charged by the EWA provider  
- Standard transfer options may be available at no cost, depending on the provider  
- Royal Lee Construction Solutions does not control or receive any portion of third-party fees  

5. Payroll Reconciliation  
- Any wages accessed through the EWA program will be automatically deducted from the employee’s next scheduled paycheck  
- Payroll statements will reflect all EWA transactions, deductions, and remaining net pay  

6. Employee Responsibility  
By enrolling in the EWA program, employees acknowledge and agree that:
- They are responsible for managing their wage access and personal financial planning  
- Early access to wages will reduce their future paycheck amounts  
- Abuse, misuse, or policy violations may result in suspension or termination of EWA access  

7. Voluntary Participation  
Participation in the EWA program is entirely voluntary. Employees are not required to enroll or utilize this benefit as a condition of employment.

8. Authorization & Consent  
By signing below, the employee authorizes Royal Lee Construction Solutions and its authorized EWA provider to:
- Verify hours worked for wage access calculations  
- Process wage advances based on approved earnings  
- Deduct any advanced wages from future payroll payments  
- Share necessary payroll, employment, and time-tracking data with the EWA provider for program administration
